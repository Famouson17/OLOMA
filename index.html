Our Story

The Journey of
Oloma

I

Roots in Akoko-Edo

Oloma is a community in Akoko-Edo Local Government Area of Edo State, Nigeria. Part of the Edoid language family, our people have deep and distinguished roots going back centuries. Our existence was first formally documented in the 19th century as part of the linguistic study of the Edoid peoples.

II

A Living Culture

Our history breathes through festivals, language, dances, and the stories of our elders. Oloma has preserved its identity through connection to the land and to each other across every generation. The Ọ́lọ̀mà language, with its rich noun class system and concord patterns, is a unique and living heritage.

III

Building the Future

Today, Oloma looks forward — nurturing youth, celebrating heritage, and building a digital presence so our story reaches every corner of the world where Oloma people live.
